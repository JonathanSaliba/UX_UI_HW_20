About Spontour

Introduction

A travel planning app that helps elevate the pain points involved with planning a trip, either alone or with a group of friends through custom/curated experiences based on the individuals' or groups' interests. The projected consisted the use of the following tools and methodologies, User Research (Interviews + Surveys) & Analysis, Persona Creation, User Experience Design (UI/UX), Wireframing, Prototyping, Remote User Testing, Figma, Marvel, and InVision.

User Insight iteraion
Iteration 1

Alex, a current student, needs a better way to plan her activities when she travels aboard with her friends because she wants to enjoy herself and not have to stress about all the details.

Iteration 2

Alex, a current life science student at University of Toronto needs a better way to coordinate and plan different activities when she travels aboard with friends because she wants to be able to enjoy herself with them while avoiding the stresses of micromanaging and coordinating all the different interests and travel styles they have.

Iteration 3

Alex, a current life science student at University of Toronto is always looking for her next adventure with friends but finds it stressful to coordinate all the different activities based on everyone's interests, and finds herself often micromanaging and compromising with everyone. She needs a better way to plan different activities when she travels because she wants to be able to enjoy herself with her friends while avoiding the stress of micromanaging the details of the trip.

Problem Statement Iteration
Iteration 1

It is difficult for travelers to coordinate with fellow travelers, design itineraries, and research/find the best activities while considering everyone's interests. Whether you’re a meticulous planner or spontaneous traveler, the planning process is riddled with stress and hard work when managing a group trip.

Iteration 2

It is difficult for travelers to manage and coordinate the complexities of designing an itinerary, researching/finding the best activities for a group of people while considering the different interests and priorities they may have. Whether you’re a meticulous planner or spontaneous traveler, the planning process is riddled with stress and hard work when managing a group trip.

Iterations Made Based On User Test

The Iterations made include the following:
Added descriptive text and notifications
Added confirm password box in sign-up page
Added “Next” text beside Navigation arrows
Added Sign in/ Sign-out button on home screen
Changed “Curate a trip” button to “We build a trip”
Added a confirm button on itinerary details page

IOS Prototype Video

Key Findings:
1.It was interesting to understand the core needs and motivations that people had when it came to planning activities when traveling.
2.Finding out that a wireframe without text vs with text can change the user experience and how easy it is for them to understand what the screens purpose is.
3.Removing yourself from the app and looking at things objectively to create a better user experience and make the most from the feedback given.
Next steps:
1.Iterate IOS prototype to be more straightforward and clear
2.Add better visuals to increase its visual appeal
3.Create screens for the Build a trip Feature
4.Adjusting frames to allow for vertical and horizontal scrolling where appropriate.
